Agaricus placomyces Peck
flat-cap Agaricus, flat-capped Psalliota

Synonyms: Psalliota placomyces (Peck) Kauffman

Description: Gilled mushroom, cap 4 - 13 cm broad; stem 6 - 12 cm long, 10 - 15 mm thick. Cap convex to flattened with a flattened center throughout development, whitish. Cap surface dry, smooth, with dark center and numerous, small, brownish-black scales decreasing in density toward margin. Cap flesh white to pink, stains yellow to brown. Gills free, crowded, white then pink, becoming chocolate brown to blackish- brown at maturity. Partial veil leaves large, pendant, persistent veil, which may have yellow-brown droplets on the underside. Stem equal or slightly tapered to apex with a small flattened bulb at its base. Stem white, bruising yellow, especially at base, smooth, sometimes with brown cottony patches on lower part of stem. Odor phenolic, of carbolic acid, ink, or creosote. Taste unpleasant. Spore print deep chocolate brown. Spores broadly elliptical; 4.5 - 6.5 x 3.5 - 4.5 µm.

Similar Species: The cap with dark center and abundant, minute, brown scales, the large ring, and bulbous stem with yellow staining at base separate this Agaricus from most other species. Agaricus xanthodermis also has phenol odor but cap margin has bright yellow staining. Agaricus xanthodermis

Fruiting: July to September.

Habitat and Ecology: Uncommon, found under hardwood or conifer trees. Single or several on the ground. Soil saprobe.

Notes: Agaricus placomyces and A. xanthodermis are two of the poisonous species in this genus.

Etymology: "Agaricon" is an ancient Greek word for fungus, reportedly derived from Agara, the name of a Greek city which had abundant mushrooms nearby. Placomyces is from the Greek "placo" meaning flat, and "mykes" for fungus.
