Agaricus xanthodermus Genevier
yellow stainer, yellow foot

Description: Cap marshmallow shaped when young, becoming convex to flat, maintaining a flat center throughout development, white with a buff-brown center caused by a higher density of flattened brown fibers or scales covering the cap. Cap margin typically staining bright yellow when rubbed. Cap surface smooth, dry, covered with flattened fibers, becoming scaly with age. Cap flesh thick, firm, white, stains bright yellow when bruised and with KOH. Gills free, close, pale pink to brown, becoming blackish brown at maturity. Partial veil leaves thick, membranous, white or yellow-stained, skirt-like ring on upper part of stem. Ring margin thickened with a double lip (top surface smooth, bottom surface cottony near margin). Stem equal or with basal bulb, smooth, white bruising yellow to brown. Stem flesh stuffed to hollow. Stem base staining bright yellow when cut. Odor and taste phenol or inky. Spore print chocolate brown. Spores elliptical, smooth; 4.5 - 6 x 3 - 4.5 µm.

Similar Species: The bright yellow staining of both the cap margin and the stem base, the phenol odor, and pale color are distinctive for this species. Agaricus placomyces has a similar odor and staining but the cap has numerous small, dark brown scales. Agaricus placomyces

Fruiting: Thus far reported for September.

Habitat and Ecology: Uncommon, found in hardwood forest, but reported for various habitats elsewhere in country. Single to grouped on the ground. Soil saprobe.

Notes: Agaricus xanthodermis and A. placomyces are two of the poisonous species in this genus.

Etymology: "Agaricon" is an ancient Greek word for fungus, reportedly derived from Agara, the name of a Greek city which had abundant mushrooms nearby. "Xanthodermis" means "yellow skin" in reference to the characteristic yellow staining.
