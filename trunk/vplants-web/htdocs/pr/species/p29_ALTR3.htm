Common Name:
Allium tricoccum Ait.
Synonyms: Validallium tricoccum (Ait.) Small

Common Names: wild leek, ramps, wild onion

Description: Perennial herb with 2 - 6 bulbs, leaves or flowering stalk 20 - 40 cm tall. Bulbs up to 6 cm tall and 3 cm wide, somewhat oval shaped, and encased in a brownish or gray netlike coating. Leaves two or three, arising from bulb apex at or near soil level, and dying back before flowering time. The leaves have a long, narrow petiole, a flat, wide blade (up to 9 cm wide), and non-toothed margins. Inflorescence an umbel of 12 - 50 flowers raised on a single narrow stalk with two bracts below the base of the flower cluster. Flowers white to creamy, less than 1 cm long, and bell-shaped. The tepals remain even when fruiting. Fruit a 3-lobed capsule. Go to Plant Glossary

Similar Species: Other native species of Allium in our area have much narrower, stalkless leaves that persist through the blooming period. Allium tricoccum is also represented by a variety, A. tricoccum var. burdickii, in the Chicago Region. The variety can be distinguished by its shorter bulbs (only 2 - 4 cm tall), which are typically buried much deeper. Allium tricoccum var. burdickii also has narrower leaf blades (1.5 - 4.5 cm wide), shorter bracts below the umbel (only 1 -2 cm long), fewer flowers in the umbel (6 - 24 flowers), and the flowering time beginning earlier in June and fruits maturing in July.
Allium tricoccum var. burdickii

Flowering: July to August.

Habitat and Ecology: Common in mesic woods, especially in the eastern part of the Chicago Region.

Notes: The name for the city of Chicago is derived from the Native American Miami-Illinois word "chicagoua," which means "striped skunk" and was also used in reference to the smelly wild leek, Allium tricoccum. The Miami-Illinois commonly named rivers after conspicuous plants in a watershed. "Chicagoua" was their name for the Chicago River. This name was later adopted for the town located at the mouth of the river, Chicago, which was incorporated in 1833.

Etymology: Allium comes from the Latin word for garlic. Tricoccum is a combination of tri meaning three, and coccum meaning berry, which refers to the 3-lobed fruit.

Regional Status: Native.

vPlants Taxon Code: ALTR3
