Stereum ostrea (Blume & T. Nees:Fr.) Fr.
Synonyms: Stereum fasciatum (Schw.) Fr.; Stereum lobatum (Kunze:Fr.) Fr.

Common Names: false turkey-tail

Description: Non-pored bracket, 1 - 7 cm broad. Brackets formed singly or in overlapping clusters. Shape thin, flat or upturned, narrowed to base, sometimes with stalk-like base. Upper surface grayish, minutely hairy, with shiny brown zones. Green coloration may develop from algae living on the upper surface. Under surface smooth (no pores) with pale reddish brown color. Go to Fungus Glossary

Similar Species: Often confused with Trametes versicolor (turkey-tail) but Trametes have pores on the underside. The other Stereum species in the Chicago region tend be more broadly attached, less dramatically zoned, or of different coloration. Trametes versicolor Stereum complicatum Stereum hirsutum Stereum striatum

Fruiting: Throughout the season and persisting over winter.

Habitat and Ecology: Common in hardwood forest, oak woodland, oak savanna, and areas with large woody debris. Found on logs and other dead wood of hardwoods. Wood saprobe causing a white rot.

Notes: The taxonomy of Stereum is complicated and research indicates that some interbreeding occurs between morphological species. This may explain why forms are found which are intermediate with Stereum hirsutum.

Etymology: Stereum derived from the Greek stereos meaning hard or stiff. Ostrea is from the Latin for oyster.

vPlants Taxon Code: STER2OSTR
